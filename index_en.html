Causal Inference：Legalization of Recreational Marijuana Could Help Reduce Crime Rates？

  • There has been a long-term debate about the pros and cons of using recreational marijuana. The pros support the legalization of marijuana could reduce the risk of black market transactions; while the opponents believe that the use of marijuana is physically harmful.
  • Dataset source：Crime Rates - The FBI's Crime Data Explorer (CDE), and household data from states - The United States Census Bureau.
  • Data cleaning by using Python (Pandas) and Excel VBA.
  • Stata (applying Synthetic Control Method) and presented by LaTeX.

Public Policy：Can Sustainability Policies Reverse a Company's ESG Performance？

  • Null Hypothesis - 1：Mandatory policies are of substantial help in improving the company's ESG indicators and actions.
  • Null Hypothesis - 2：Companies that voluntarily participate in the submission of ESG sustainability reports will have better ESG performance.
  • Used Difference in Difference Method (DID) to infer the causal relationship on "how the policies could actually influence the compaines behaviors".
  • Data source：TEJ (Taiwan Economic Journals), Market Observe Post System.
  • using Python (Pandas - data cleaning and analysis, SciPy - Statistics and Regression, Matplotlib - plotting), and Tableau as final presentation.
